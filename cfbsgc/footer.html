2D Descriptors:
- Aggregator Advisor: highest tanimoto coefficient to known protein aggregation-inducing compound (http://advisor.bkslab.org/index.php/faq).
- BalabanJ: distance-based topological index (uses distances sums for vertices); Chem. Phys. Lett. 89:399-404 (1982).
- BertzCT: complexity index, taking into account both the variety of kinds of bond connectivities and atom types; J. Am. Chem. Soc. 103:3599-601 (1981).
- Ipc: information for graph polynomial coefficients-based information theory; J. Chem. Phys. 67:4517-33 (1977).
- HallKierAlpha: alpha modified kappa shape index; (https://www.chemcomp.com/journal/descr.htm#KH); Rev. Comput. Chem. 2:367-422 (1991).
- Kappa1 - Kappa3: kappa shape indices; (https://www.chemcomp.com/journal/descr.htm#KH); Rev. Comput. Chem. 2:367-422 (1991).
- Chi0, Chi1, Chi0n - Chi4n, Chi0v - Chi4v: connectivity indices; (https://www.chemcomp.com/journal/descr.htm#KH); Rev. Comput. Chem. 2:367-422 (1991).
- MolLogP: log of the Octanol:Water partition coefficient; Wildman and Crippen JCICS 39:868-73 (1999).
- MolMR: Molecular Refractivity; Wildman and Crippen JCICS 39:868-73 (1999).
- NumSpiroAtoms: number of atoms shared between rings that share exactly one atom.
- NumBridgeheadAtoms: number of atoms shared between rings that share at least two bonds.
- TPSA: topological polar surface area; J. Med. Chem. 43:3714-7, (2000).
- LabuteASA: Labute's Approximate Surface Area; J. Mol. Graph. Mod. 18:464-77 (2000).
- PEOE_VSA1 - PEOE_VSA14: MOE-type descriptors using partial charges and surface area contributions; http://www.chemcomp.com/journal/vsadesc.htm
- SMR_VSA1 - SMR_VSA10: MOE-type descriptors using MR contributions and surface area contributions; http://www.chemcomp.com/journal/vsadesc.htm
- SlogP_VSA1 - SlogP_VSA12: MOE-type descriptors using LogP contributions and surface area contributions; http://www.chemcomp.com/journal/vsadesc.htm
- EState_VSA1 - EState_VSA11; VSA_EState1 - VSA_EState10: MOE-type descriptors using EState indices and surface area contributions (unpublished, developed by RD)
